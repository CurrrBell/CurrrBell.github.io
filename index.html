University of Rochester

Lead Workshop Leader

Workshops are a substitution for the traditional recitation model that the University is very proud of. The idea is to increase student engagement by allowing them to take an active role in their learning rather than a passive one. Students spend all of their time in workshops in groups with other students rather than being lectured to by a TA. As a workshop leader, it was my job to facilitate these interactions; it was not my job to teach them the material. I would instead act as a guide through the weekly problems, providing clues when needed and encouraging quieter students to participate. In my third semester as a workshop leader I assumed a lead role for an introductory programming class. In addition to my normal workshop leader duties, I assisted newer workshop leaders with keeping their workshops healthy and productive.
